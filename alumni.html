Dhoni was born in Ranchi, Bihar (now in Jharkhand) and hails from a Hindu family with roots in Uttarakhand.[15][16] He is the youngest of three children of Pan Singh and Devaki Devi.[17][18][19][20] His paternal village Lwali, is in Jainti Tehsil, Lamgara block of the Almora District of Uttarakhand.[21] His parents moved from Uttarakhand to Ranchi, Jharkhand where his father worked as a pump operator in junior management position in MECON Colony situated in Doranda area in Ranchi.[22] Unlike Dhoni, his uncle and cousins spell their surname as Dhauni.[21][needs copy edit] Previously Dhoni was the goalkeeper for his DAV Jawahar Vidya Mandir school's football team,[23] but after seeing his goalkeeping skills, coach Keshav Ranjan Banerjee, one who inspired Dhoni to be a cricketer, picked him to play cricket for his school team.[24] His exceptional wicketkeeping skills allowed him to become the regular wicketkeeper at the Commando Cricket Club (1995â€“1998).
